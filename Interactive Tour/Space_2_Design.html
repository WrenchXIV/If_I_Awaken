The spatial move

A courtyard with a horizon

Audience enters from the bottom-right, led by the Tour Guide through a row of tianguis stalls until they land in The Courtyard — a central plaza ringed by six stone tables (round pink-stone tops on green pedestals). The band stage sits to their north; the south mural wall is their horizon. Scenic graduates outward — smallest in the center, largest at the edges — so sight lines from the Courtyard stay clean. During the youth poem, audience faces north (band stage); during the elder poem, they turn 180° to face south (mural wall). The elder walks forward through the scenic elements he names and ends his poem in the middle of the Courtyard, surrounded by the audience, with the Youth Poet joining him there.

03 · Scene Sequence

Ten beats of the neighborhood

01
The tunnel opens
ProcessionWarm wash Audience emerges from the transition tunnel into the east aisle of Space 2. They smell the tianguis before they see it — spice, carne asada, cilantro in the air. The Tour Guide leads.
02
Through the market
Tour Guide liveScenic walkDancers as vendors Tour Guide, gesturing grandly: "Welcome to Boyle Heights! Home of El Mercadito." He leads the audience through a row of five tianguis stalls — each vendor played by one of the dancers in costume, hailing them, gesturing at their wares. They pass the lowrider, the taco stand, the paletero cart. The scenic gets smaller as they move inward.
03
The Courtyard
SettlePlaza wash Audience arrives at The Courtyard. Six stone tables ring the space — round pink-stone tops on green pedestals, with curved concrete benches around each. The band plays quietly on the stage to the north. A warm wash holds the square.
04
The speech
Tour Guide live Tour Guide: "Let's stop here in this courtyard for a moment. Feel free to take a seat at one of the surrounding benches, but only if you need to. Because you are now standing in the epicenter of Los Angeles' Chicano culture. Stretching all the way from the iconic intersections of San Vicente & La Cienaga…" (He struggles to pronounce the names.)
05
The reveal
Voice from band stageSpot snaps upBack-and-forth banter A voice from behind the audience, crisp and correct: "You mean San Vicente and La Cienaga." The spotlight on the band stage snaps up — the Youth Poet has been there all along. The audience turns to see her. She and the Tour Guide volley for a moment — her sharp, him sheepish. Finally he cedes the stage and exits.
06
Youth on the stage
Spot tightensBand playing underProjection billboards live The band softens under her. She begins her poem. The two billboards flanking her come alive with projected images of the places she names — the 7-Eleven on Osborne, Mariachi Square, Van Nuys Blvd, Reseda, Sylmar, the 210 freeway curving through the hills. Audience faces north, transfixed.
07
Her spot goes out
Blackout on poetHeld silence "We sprouted from the soil / And the soil remembers / Us." Her spot snaps out. The billboards go dark. The band holds a chord. A beat of silence.
08
Voice from the south
Voice from muralAudience 180° From the other side of the room — from the mural wall behind them — a new voice: "Los Angeles is a tianguis, a marvelous marketplace…" The audience turns 180°. A follow spot finds the Elder Poet at the base of the mural.
09
The elder walks in
Elder walks through spaceLights chase elements The Elder begins walking forward through the scenic elements as he names them. Lighting chases his path — the lowrider lights up when he speaks of "lowriding is where cars become moving museums"; the tianguis stalls glow when he says "where wisdom, song, and teachings go with the tamales"; the hanging merchandise catches color on "art is alive on the walls on skin and cars." The room is his illustration. His walk carries him steadily toward the Courtyard.
10
Final words in the Courtyard
Audience partsYouth descends · Elder finishes The Elder reaches the edge of the crowd. The audience parts and he walks into the middle of the Courtyard. The Youth Poet steps down from the band stage and joins him there. He delivers the final lines of his poem from the center of the crowd — surrounded by the audience, Youth Poet beside him. The section ends with both poets standing floor-level in the middle of the Courtyard. Blackout holds briefly before the transition.
04 · Design State

What's locked · what's open

Locked

  • The Courtyard: central audience gathering · widened to ~34 ft diameter for 150 pax
  • Six stone tables ring the Courtyard · tianguis-style seating for those who need it
  • Tour Guide leads processional from bottom-right entry through tianguis stalls
  • Warehouse ceiling stays exposed · stage lights only · no overhead decor
  • Band stage: 20 ft diameter · 3 ft elevated · shared with 3/4/5 · 1/4 in Space 2
  • 10 ft inner circle for 5-piece jazz · 5 ft outer ring for up to 6 dancers/performers
  • South warehouse wall: hybrid trompe-l'oeil / street art mural
  • Projection billboards flanking band stage for youth poem
  • Full-scale props: lowrider, taco stand, bus bench, paletero cart, payphone
  • Five tianguis stalls line the entry path — vendors played by the 5 dancers in costume
  • Youth Poet pre-placed on band stage · revealed by spotlight on interruption
  • Elder poem processional from mural wall · ends in the middle of the Courtyard with Youth Poet joining him

Still open

  • Mural content specifics — what neighborhood view, what street-art imagery overlaid?
  • Exact projection content for billboards during youth poem
  • What's sold/displayed at the tianguis stalls
  • Lowrider specifics — vintage, hydraulics practical or static, paint treatment
  • Is the Tour Guide mic'd hands-free or with a handheld mic?
  • Transition to Space 3 — does the Tour Guide lead them, or does lighting pull them?
