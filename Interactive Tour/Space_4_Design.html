If I Awaken in Los Angeles · Space 04 of 06

Black LA — a journey through time

Anchored by a full church set on the west wall, then a journey through four zones of Black Los Angeles — the Great Migration, the Central Ave jazz era, the birth of hip-hop, and Leimert Park. The poet speaks from a fixed band stage; the audience travels through the history around her, guided by dancers with two dance breaks built into the poem.

Church · Migration · Jazz Era · Birth of Hip Hop · Leimert Park

The section begins with the audience gathered facing west — three rows of pews backed by an altar rail — watching a choir perform against a full church set. On special nights, a live 5–8 person choir; most nights, a pre-recorded choir video plays on a 14 ft × 9 ft LED screen mounted on the church's back wall, with audio over speakers. A small functional elevated stage (18 ft × 12 ft × 1 ft) sits in front of the LED so a choir can perform there when available.

When the choir piece ends, the dancers spill onto the church stage for a brief dance break. Lights go out on the church. A spotlight snaps up on the band stage to the east, revealing a single poet. The audience turns 180° and follows her voice. As the poem moves, dancers usher the audience through four zones — Migration, Jazz Era, Birth of Hip Hop, Leimert Park. Two dance breaks are built into the poem: the poet pauses in the Jazz zone for a swing-dance number (dancers pull audience members in to dance), and again in the Hip Hop zone for a breakdance circle (dancers gather the audience around them). The Leimert Park zone sits closest to the band stage — where the poem lands, the drum circle plays, and the audience ends the section.

02Audience perspective
Two pivotal moments
VIEW A · THE CHURCH "I KNOW IT WAS THE BLOOD"

Audience facing west. The LED screen plays a choir video; on special nights, a live choir fills the stage in front of it. Three rows of pews in front of the audience, altar rail behind, candle markers extending into the distance — the boundary of the holy space.

VIEW B · THE HIP HOP BREAK "BIRTHED HIP-HOP FROM HEARTACHE"

The poet has paused. Record scratch. The beat drops hard. In the middle of the audience circle, a dancer hits a freeze — the other four dancers hype around him. The audience has formed a natural ring. The poet stands lit by a distant teal spot on the band stage, waiting for the last freeze before she picks the poem back up.

03Beat sequence
10 beats · ~12–14 minutes
01
Enter the church
Tour Guide liveProcession from Space 3 As the five dancers hold their last pose in Space 3, the Tour Guide returns to the audience — hyped, winded, grinning: "Whew! Now THAT's what I call a show — five towns, one breath, somebody hand me a glass of water!" He gathers them and starts walking east. "But we've still got ground to cover. Come with me this way…" As they cross into Space 4, his voice drops into something more reverent, more building, and he lands the line that sets the whole next section: "We're going to take you to church."

Audience faces west. The full church set is waiting — stage, altar rail, a soft tungsten wash. The LED screen is dark. The Tour Guide, gentler now, invites them in: "Alright, alright. There are some seats in the front for anyone who needs to sit down for a moment. For everyone else, gather around. Service is just about to get started…"
02
The choir · "I Know It Was The Blood"
LED live · warm church washChoir audio over speakers The LED screen lights up with the choir video (or on special nights, the live 5–8 person choir takes the stage). Audio fills the room. "One day when I was lost, he died upon the cross / And I know it was the blood for me." The audience stands in reverence inside the boundary of pews, altar rail, and candles.
03
Dancers join · the church comes alive
5 dancers on church stageCelebratory break As the choir hits its closing crescendo, the five dancers emerge from the wings onto the church stage. A brief, joyful dance break — call-and-response movement with the choir video, hands to the sky, gospel energy. Audience hands clap, some sway.
04
The pivot · poet's opening · call to follow
Church blackoutHeld silence → guitarPoet's opening stanzasAudience turns 180° Lights cut on the church. Full blackout on the west wall. A single spotlight snaps up on the band stage to the east — and there, revealed, stands the Poet. The audience turns 180° as one. She delivers the first stanzas — "Welcome back, to the City of Angels! / Where we got equal angles / The hood an isosceles / Half light / Half dark / Gangstas spit wisdom like Socrates…" — still holding the audience in the church zone, letting them find her voice.

She lands the invitation: "…it seems I have your ear for a while. So let me tell you how our culture made this town." She pauses. The band's guitarist rolls in with a slow, rooted Leadbelly-style progression. The Tour Guide steps forward beside the dancers and picks up the thread: "Come on, y'all — she's about to take us back. Follow us, right this way…" The dancers offer their hands. The journey east begins.
05
Zone 1 · Migration · dancers usher the audience through
Poet from band stageZone 1 lights up · sepia amberDancers lead audience to zone As the audience crosses into Zone 1, the Poet picks back up — "In 1865, The 13th Amendment freed the slaves… 6 million freemen and women set off on The Great Migration." Zone 1 lights up in sepia amber around them. Suitcases at their feet. The "GO WEST" sign overhead. The chorus lands as they arrive: "I AM ALIVE IN LOS ANGELES."
06
Zone 2 · Jazz Era · the swing dance break
Poet pauses · band plays swingZone 2 lights up · golden washDancers swing · audience joins Audience settles into Zone 2. Poet reaches the line "In the 1920s, South Central was singin…" — and pauses. The band lifts: swing music pours out. The five dancers break into a full swing-dance number in front of the Dunbar marquee. Mid-number, the dancers break the fourth wall and pull audience members in — partnered lindy, easy moves, laughter. A true communal moment. When the music resolves, the band drops to a soft bed and the poet resumes: "Jazz notes jumped down Central Ave… Duke Ellington played at Dunbar and Jack's Basket Room, too… All rhythm, no blues."
07
Transit · dancers lead east
Poet continuesAudience walks east Poet threads through the walking material between zones. As dancers guide the audience toward Zone 3, she carries them through "60 years later, they presented the breaks…" setting up the next break.
08
Zone 3 · Birth of Hip Hop · the breakdance circle
Poet pauses · beat dropsZone 3 lights up · hard redRecord scratch · hip-hop drumsDancers form circle · audience rings them Audience arrives in Zone 3. Poet hits "They presented the breaks…" — and pauses. Record scratch. A classic hip-hop beat drops hard from the band stage. The five dancers gather in the middle of the space, audience forming a circle around them. Breakdance cypher: top rock, down rock, power moves, freezes. Each dancer takes a turn in the middle. When the last freeze lands, the poet picks up: "Put a beat to their pain / Birthed hip-hop from heartache… World Class Wreckin Cru played at Eve's After Dark…" Poem continues as dancers reform into ushers.
09
Zone 4 · Leimert Park · the drum circle finale
Poet final stanzasZone 4 · deep green · Sankofa glowsLive congas from bandAudience gathers close to band stage Dancers lead the audience to the center of the space, closest to the band stage — Zone 4, dressed as Leimert Park. The Sankofa symbol glows on the floor; the Leimert Park Village blade sign stands vertical at the edge; live congas kick in from the band. Poet: "Feel the rhythm of Leimert? The Village. The People… Sankofa symbols cover the pavement. 'Go back and get it.'" The poem's seven principles land here — "Unity, self-determination, collective work and responsibility, cooperative economics, purpose, creativity… Faith." Audience and dancers end gathered close around the drum circle.
10
Transition to Space 5
Tour Guide returnsExit east to Folk LA Drum circle swells and slowly fades. Poet final lines. Tour Guide steps in and ushers the audience east toward Space 5 (Folk LA). Dancers remain at the Leimert Park zone, slowly slowing, until the last audience member passes through the east threshold.
