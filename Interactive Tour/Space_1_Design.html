Space 1
Arrival & LED Introduction

Hollywood Boulevard as a wide, shallow stage. The audience stands on the Walk of Fame. The wall is the street. Dancers-as-tourists fill the sidewalk. The tour begins — and then breaks open.

New layer · dancers as tourists

The sidewalk is alive

The five dancers arrive in Space 1 costumed as tourists — cameras, maps, fanny packs, phones held high. They greet and direct the audience at arrival, move through the sidewalk as Sekou speaks, and after the blackout, return in a line along the sidewalk for the opening dance number. The audience is absorbed into the crowd: we are all tourists here, until the show decides we aren't anymore.

03 · Scene Sequence

Eight beats of the opening

01
Arrival
HOUSE UPTOURISTS GREET Audience enters from the lobby. The five dancers are already in the room, costumed as tourists — cameras around necks, fanny packs, maps, phones up. They warmly greet and direct the audience to find spots along the east wall. The theatrical convention is established without a word: we're all tourists here. Walk of Fame stars glow on the floor. LED wall holds a slow Hollywood Boulevard establishing shot.
02
Sekou enters
SPOTTCL CHINESE THEATREPHOTOS Spot finds the Tour Guide. "Welcome aboard, folks!" The wall pushes in on TCL Chinese Theatre — pagoda facade, cement handprints. Music: Hollywood Swinging. The tourist-dancers begin moving on the sidewalk — taking selfies, consulting maps, pointing at landmarks on the LED.
03
Landmarks cycle
DOLBY → WALK OF FAME → CAPITOL → HOLLYWOOD SIGNREACT As the Tour Guide narrates, the LED moves through landmark POVs. The tourists react in character — photographing the Hollywood Sign, miming shock at the stars underfoot, posing under Capitol Records, waving a friend into frame. The sidewalk is full of life.
04
The pivot
MELLOWSTREET-LEVEL FACESSLOW Music softens. Tour Guide: "I want you to look deeper." Landmarks recede. The LED lingers on real street-level faces — the fruit vendor, the auditioning woman. The tourists slow their motion. Cameras drop. Maps fold. They start actually looking.
05
The descent
PAVEMENT → SOIL → ROOTSSTARS FADE "Past the pavement, past the glamour, down into the very soil." LED camera pushes down through the sidewalk. Concrete cracks. Earth. Roots. Walk of Fame stars dim underfoot. The tourists stand still, arrested.
06
Blackout
BLACKOUTDARK LED wall goes black. Floor goes dark. The room holds a beat of silence. The tour is over.
07
The number
LIGHTS UPLINE · DANCEVIDEOAUDIO OVER SPEAKERS Lights rise. The five tourist-dancers are revealed in a line, spread along the sidewalk between the audience and the LED wall — poised, ready. A video plays on the wall; the song plays over the room's speakers. The dancers perform in front of the wall — no live poets, no live band in this space, just video, audio, and movement. The wall is the set; the dancers are the story.
08
Transition to Tunnel
WASHPATH LIT The Tour Guide returns. "Alright folks! Follow me…" A lit path on the floor draws the audience out of the room to the left, through a transition tunnel, and into the main show space. The room empties. The LED wall goes dark and stays dark until the audience returns at the very end.
04 · Design State

What's locked · what's open

Locked

  • Hollywood Blvd sidewalk setup · audience strung along LED wall
  • Floor: projection-mapped Walk of Fame (changeable)
  • LED POV: across the street · curb + street + opposite sidewalk + landmark
  • Landmark cycle: TCL → Dolby → Walk of Fame → Capitol → Hollywood Sign
  • "Look deeper" pivot → camera dives into pavement → soil → roots → blackout
  • Dancers costumed as tourists · greet and direct audience on arrival
  • Tourists live on the sidewalk during Sekou's speech — photos, maps, pointing
  • Post-blackout: dancers revealed in a line on the sidewalk for the number
  • The number: LED video + song over speakers + dance in front of wall (no live poets, no live band in this space)
  • No physical scenery — LED + projection + lighting do all the work
  • Finale reprise: floor stars become real Angeleno names

Still open

  • Song choice for the number — "If I Awaken in LA" or "The Land Remembers Us"
  • What the video on the wall shows during the number (depends on song)
  • Tourist costume aesthetic — broad/comedic or contemporary/real?
  • Do the tourists transform during the dance — shed cameras/maps, become Angelenos — or stay tourists throughout?
  • LED content during the finale reprise
  • Full transformed look of Space 1 at show's end
  • Lighting palette for the Hollywood Blvd cycle — dusk, golden hour, or neon night?
