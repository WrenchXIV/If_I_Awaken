If I Awaken in Los Angeles · Space 05 of 06

Folk LA — Topanga & Laurel Canyon

A concentric world — canyon on the outside, a '60s/'70s Laurel Canyon living room at the heart. One singer-songwriter with an acoustic guitar performs from the center. Audience surrounds him through the living room and out into the canyon, close enough to feel the strings.

Canyon ring · Living room ring · Performer center

Audience is invited over from the west through a front door — wood paneled, a stained-glass panel in the upper half, a potted plant on each side, a welcome mat, warm porch light above, a little brass house number plate. The Tour Guide knocks; the door opens; they step through into someone's home. As they cross the threshold, the temperature of the light shifts from the ancestral green of Leimert Park to golden hour amber. They find themselves in a Laurel Canyon living room with soft carpet flooring throughout — layered shag rug, multiple couches arranged in a circle facing inward, armchairs, bean bags, coffee and side tables, record player, paired vintage speakers, bookshelf, guitar stand, macramé plant hangers, floor and table lamps, floor cushions. Surrounding the home, the Topanga canyon is still visible on all sides — hanging branches, string lights, wildflowers, boulders, native trees (coast live oak, California fan palm, sycamore, Joshua tree), succulents (agave, yucca, prickly pear), and dried chaparral brush. Painted murals of the Topanga hills (north wall) and a Laurel Canyon sunset (east wall) extend the landscape out to the warehouse walls. At the heart of the living room sits the Performer — one singer-songwriter with an acoustic guitar on a wooden stool, already quietly fingerpicking as the audience arrives.

The Tour Guide and the five dancers lead the audience directly into the center of the living room, sit down cross-legged on the carpet in a loose circle around the Performer, and invite the audience to sit on the carpet, sit on the couches, or stand in any of the rings. The Performer plays through the entire Folk LA arc from the center. The section ends with the final "Folk is a seed" reprise as an audience engagement moment (writer to define), then the Tour Guide leads the audience out through the NE corner toward Space 6 — the stage, the finale.

02Audience perspective
Two moments
VIEW A · THE FRONT DOOR "SHOULD WE GO?"

The Tour Guide knocks. The door swings open — wood paneling, stained glass glowing amber, brass handle. Beyond, the warm living room and the Performer already mid-fingerpick. Potted plants flank the porch, the welcome mat waits, the porch light glows. We've just arrived at a cool friend's house in the canyon.

VIEW B · INSIDE THE LIVING ROOM "FOLK IS A SEED"

Audience is close. Some sit on the couches — those who got there first. Others stand on the shag rug around the Performer. Candles flicker on the coffee table. String lights drape overhead. Hanging branches frame the edges. The Performer plays from his wooden stool, guitar on his lap. Pure Laurel Canyon.

03Beat sequence
11 beats · ~18–22 minutes
01
The invitation · the door · circle up
Tour Guide liveAudience knocks at the door · enters a homeShift to golden hour amber As the Leimert Park drum circle fades, the Tour Guide pulls out his phone with a surprised little laugh, looks up, and turns to the audience: "I just got a text from a guy I met last week. He just asked if we want to come over, listen to some cool music… Very cool guy. He lives in the canyons. Plays guitar. Loves Joni Mitchell… what do you think? Should we go?" He leads the audience east — through the canyon — up to a wood-paneled front door with a stained-glass panel and a warm porch light above it. He knocks, and the door swings open. Inside, the Performer is already seated at the center of a cozy living room, acoustic guitar on his lap, quietly fingerpicking. Golden-hour amber. String lights twinkle overhead. The Tour Guide walks the audience straight into the center — past the couches and armchairs, onto the soft carpet — and gathers everyone close: "Come on in. This is somebody's living room. We're gonna do this the old way tonight — sit down, get comfortable, and just listen." He sits first, cross-legged on the carpet. The five dancers follow, each finding a spot around the Performer, forming a loose circle. The Tour Guide motions to the audience: "Join us. Sit on the floor, take a couch, lean on a friend — whatever feels right." Audience settles — many cross-legged on the carpet, some on couches and bean bags, the rest standing in the living room and canyon rings. The room quiets.
02
First section · "There was a moment in time" · first chorus
Performer plays + speaksSolo acoustic guitar The Performer raises his eyes. The room is his. He opens with "There was a moment in time…" and plays through the full first section — Pete Seeger and the American folk movement, then into the Laurel Canyon naming (Neil, Joni, Eagles, Jim, Judy) — and lands the first full chorus. Last line hangs: "…no matter how many times it's heard."
03
The pause · at the record player
Performer walks to record playerCasual break · conversational The Performer lets the silence sit. He sets his guitar down on the stool and rises — easy, unhurried. He walks to the record player, crouches, and starts flipping through the stack of records on the floor. He talks to the audience while he does — pulling one out, holding it up, remembering where he was the first time he heard it. Audience leans in. He glances at the band stage.
04
The invitation · "Hey band…"
Performer picks guitar back upBrief guitar figure, then silence The Performer returns to his stool, picks up the guitar, and plays a brief phrase — just a few bars. Then stops. He cocks his head, smiles, and calls across the room toward the band stage in the SW corner: "Hey band. This feels too good to do alone. Y'all wanna come over to my place and jam?" A beat. Cheers and laughter from the band stage.
05
The band joins the circle
5-piece band crosses inBand picks up living room instruments · sits on floorWarmth spreads through whole room The five band members step down off their stage, instruments in hand, and walk across the canyon ring into the living room. As they arrive, they see the instruments scattered around — the extra guitars on the stand, a tambourine on a side table, a harmonica on the coffee table, maracas on a couch, a hand drum by a bean bag. Each band member swaps or adds an instrument to what they already carry. They find spots on the carpet around the Performer — one beside the Tour Guide, two with the dancers, one leaning against a couch. It's not a concert anymore — it's a jam session in someone's home. The Performer nods. They all start playing together.
06
Canter's, Troubadour, Tower Records
Performer + band jammingRecord player picks up a spot The group settles into a warm rolling groove. The Performer picks the thread back up: "There was a moment in time…" — and takes the audience through Canter's Deli, the Troubadour, Tower Records, and the lost ritual of sitting on the floor listening to a whole record end to end. The record player beside him catches a spotlight, platter slowly spinning, as he lands the section: "…until the record ends."
07
The psychedelic rabbit hole
Performer continuesString lights pulse color · amber → magenta → greenBand drifts into psychedelic texture The band's groove opens out — dreamier, stranger. String lights overhead shift color slowly — amber bleeds into magenta, into green, back. The Performer guides the audience through the Leary-and-Alpert era — the Harvard psychedelic club, the rabbit hole, the guru, the frontier. He lands on the question: "Could the Harvard psychedelic club give birth to the summer of love without the drugs?"
08
Ash Grove · the roots of American music
Performer continuesAmber returns · single warm poolBand drops to rootsy quiet Colors settle. Amber returns. The band drops into a quieter, rootsier feel — a blues walking line, brushed snare, plucked mandolin. The Performer invokes the Ash Grove — hillbilly ballads, gospel, blues, folk Black and White. He lands it with weight: "…when Ed Pearl dimmed the lights, the roots of American music came to life." A reverent pause. The room is still.
09
End of the era · Beach Boys to Zeppelin
Performer continuesGroove hardens · rock edges inCandles low · string lights dim The band's groove hardens — electric guitar leaks in, drums get heavier. The mood tightens. The Performer opens with "The Beach Boys, surf rock, and the British Invasion — the inevitable end to the endless summer vacation…" and narrates the turn — times changing fast, Manson taking the innocence, psychedelics to speed, Elvis and Cash fading. He lands it on: "…the belting falsetto of Robert Plant."
10
Final chorus · "Folk is a seed" · audience engagement event
Performer + band + audience singFull room harmonyAudience engagement event · writer to define The band eases back. The Performer takes the chorus again — open, warm, inviting: "Folk is a seed…" He turns the song outward. The dancers and the Tour Guide, still on the carpet, begin to hum the response line. [Audience engagement event — mechanic TBD by writer: could be hand-to-hand, circle-up, candle-pass, call-and-response hum, or movement-based sharing.] The whole room is in the song — a circle of people in someone's living room, singing together. The Performer lands the final line: "…singing together is the only medicine for the pain of one human voice." Held. The last chord rings.
11
Transition to Space 6 · the finale
Tour Guide stands · speaksExit NE corner · performer + band follow later The chord fades. For a long breath nobody moves. Then the Tour Guide rises slowly from the carpet, takes a look around the room, and grins: "This was great. I could hang out here and make music all night. But sorry, [Performer] — we've got one more stop on our tour of Los Angeles." He motions for the audience to stand and follow. He leads them out through the NE corner of the space — toward the stage room. The Performer and band remain in the circle, quietly holding the room until the last audience member crosses the threshold — then they'll rise and follow for the finale.
