The argument

Los Angeles has spent a century selling itself as a billboard. If I Awaken in Los Angeles is the show that walks behind the sign — through Boyle Heights and Koreatown, Leimert Park and Laurel Canyon, Filipinotown and the South Central church — and finds the city the postcards never printed.

The real LA is not on the hill.
It's in this room.

Why the scenic spend

The room is the show.

Every dollar of scenic design goes into something the audience walks through, not past. Seven distinct neighborhoods, built once and inhabited for fourteen months.

7
Built environments — each its own neighborhood, its own scenic language, its own crowd geometry.
110ft
LED wall in Space 01 — the longest single video surface in any LA immersive production.
150
Audience members per show, walking through together. The crowd is part of the staging.
14mo
Run length. The build amortizes over hundreds of performances, not a single tour stop.

What makes it important
The sidewalk is alive.

The five dancers arrive in Space 1 costumed as tourists — cameras, maps, fanny packs, phones held high. They greet and direct the audience at arrival, move through the sidewalk as Sekou speaks, and after the blackout, return in a line for the opening dance number. We are all tourists here, until the show decides we aren't anymore.

What makes it important
A courtyard with a horizon.

Audience enters from the bottom-right, led through a row of tianguis stalls until they land in The Courtyard — a central plaza ringed by six stone tables. The band stage sits to their north; the mural wall is their horizon. Scenic graduates outward — smallest in the center, largest at the edges — so sight lines stay clean. Audience faces north for the youth poet, then turns 180° to face the elder.
