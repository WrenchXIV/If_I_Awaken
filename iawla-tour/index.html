Front-of-house · pre-show
The arrival
Thirty minutes before curtain, the audience steps off the street into a Hollywood premiere, then drifts into a brick-and-velvet bar that already feels like the after-party.
Space 01
Hollywood Boulevard
Hollywood Boulevard as a wide, shallow stage. The audience stands on the Walk of Fame. The wall is the street. Dancers-as-tourists fill the sidewalk. The tour begins — and then breaks open.
01 · Arrival
HOUSE UP · TOURISTS GREET

Audience enters from the lobby. The five dancers are already in the room, costumed as tourists — cameras around necks, fanny packs, maps, phones up. They warmly greet and direct the audience to find spots along the east wall. The theatrical convention is established without a word: we're all tourists here.

02 · Sekou enters
SPOT · TCL CHINESE THEATRE · PHOTOS

Spot finds the Tour Guide. "Welcome aboard, folks!" The wall pushes in on TCL Chinese Theatre — pagoda facade, cement handprints. Hollywood Swinging. The tourist-dancers take selfies, consult maps, point at landmarks on the LED.

03 · Landmarks cycle
DOLBY → WALK OF FAME → CAPITOL → HOLLYWOOD SIGN

As the Tour Guide narrates, the LED moves through landmark POVs. The tourists react in character — photographing the Hollywood Sign, miming shock at the stars underfoot, posing under Capitol Records. The sidewalk is full of life.

04 · The pivot
MELLOW · STREET-LEVEL FACES · SLOW

Music softens. "I want you to look deeper." Landmarks recede. The LED lingers on real street-level faces — the fruit vendor, the auditioning woman. The tourists slow their motion. Cameras drop. Maps fold. They start actually looking.

05 · The descent
PAVEMENT → SOIL → ROOTS · STARS FADE

"Past the pavement, past the glamour, down into the very soil." LED camera pushes down through the sidewalk. Concrete cracks. Earth. Roots. Walk of Fame stars dim underfoot. The tourists stand still, arrested.

06 · Blackout
DARK · SILENCE · HOLD
07 · The number
LIGHTS UP · LINE · DANCE · VIDEO · AUDIO

The five tourist-dancers are revealed in a line, spread along the sidewalk between the audience and the LED wall. A video plays on the wall; the song plays over the room's speakers. The dancers perform — no live poets, no live band, just video, audio, and movement. The wall is the set; the dancers are the story.

08 · To the tunnel
WASH · PATH LIT

The Tour Guide returns. "Alright folks! Follow me…" A lit path on the floor draws the audience out of the room to the left, through a transition tunnel.

Space 02
Boyle Heights
A warehouse tianguis. Scenic elements grow larger as they move outward from a central courtyard. The youth poet sings from the band stage; the elder poet walks in from the mural wall; they meet in the middle.
01 · The tunnel opens
PROCESSION · WARM WASH

Audience emerges from the transition tunnel into the east aisle. They smell the tianguis before they see it — spice, carne asada, cilantro in the air. The Tour Guide leads.

02 · Through the market
SCENIC WALK · DANCERS AS VENDORS

"Welcome to Boyle Heights! Home of El Mercadito." He leads through five tianguis stalls — each vendor played by a dancer in costume, hailing them, gesturing at their wares. Lowrider, taco stand, paletero cart. The scenic gets smaller as they move inward.

03 · The Courtyard
SETTLE · PLAZA WASH

Six stone tables ring the space — round pink-stone tops on green pedestals, with curved concrete benches. The band plays quietly on the stage to the north. A warm wash holds the square.

04 · The speech
TOUR GUIDE LIVE

"Let's stop here in this courtyard for a moment. Feel free to take a seat at one of the surrounding benches, but only if you need to. Because you are now standing in the epicenter of Los Angeles' Chicano culture. Stretching all the way from the iconic intersections of San Vicente & La Cienaga…" (He struggles to pronounce the names.)

05 · The reveal
VOICE · SPOT SNAPS UP · BANTER

A voice from behind, crisp and correct: "You mean San Vicente and La Cienaga." The spotlight on the band stage snaps up — the Youth Poet has been there all along. The audience turns. She and the Tour Guide volley for a moment — her sharp, him sheepish. Finally he cedes the stage and exits.

06 · Youth on the stage
SPOT TIGHTENS · BAND · BILLBOARDS LIVE

The band softens. She begins her poem. The two billboards flanking her come alive with projected images — the 7-Eleven on Osborne, Mariachi Square, Van Nuys Blvd, Reseda, Sylmar, the 210 freeway curving through the hills. Audience faces north, transfixed.

07 · Her spot goes out
BLACKOUT ON POET · HELD SILENCE

"We sprouted from the soil / And the soil remembers / Us." Her spot snaps out. The billboards go dark. The band holds a chord. A beat of silence.

08 · Voice from the south
VOICE FROM MURAL · AUDIENCE 180°

From the mural wall behind them, a new voice: "Los Angeles is a tianguis, a marvelous marketplace…" The audience turns 180°. A follow spot finds the Elder Poet at the base of the mural.

09 · The elder walks in
ELDER WALKS · LIGHTS CHASE ELEMENTS

The Elder walks forward through the scenic elements as he names them. The lowrider lights up — "lowriding is where cars become moving museums." The tianguis stalls glow — "where wisdom, song, and teachings go with the tamales." The hanging merchandise catches color — "art is alive on the walls on skin and cars."

10 · Final words
AUDIENCE PARTS · YOUTH DESCENDS · ELDER FINISHES

The audience parts and he walks into the middle of the Courtyard. The Youth Poet steps down from the band stage and joins him. He delivers the final lines from the center of the crowd — surrounded by the audience, Youth Poet beside him. Blackout holds briefly before the transition.
