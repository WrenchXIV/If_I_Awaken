Lobby · Pre-show

The arrival

Thirty minutes before curtain. Off the street, into a Hollywood premiere, then a brick-and-velvet bar that already feels like the after-party.

PRESS & HOLD anywhere — take a breath

Space 01

Hollywood Boulevard

A wide, shallow stage. The audience stands on the Walk of Fame. The wall is the street.

01 · House up · Tourists greet

Arrival

The five dancers, costumed as tourists, greet you and direct you to the east wall. Without a word: we're all tourists here.

TAP each tourist who greets you (5)
02 · Spot · TCL Chinese Theatre

Sekou enters

"Welcome aboard, folks!" Spot finds the Tour Guide. The wall pushes in on TCL Chinese Theatre — pagoda facade, cement handprints. Hollywood Swinging.

TAP the spotlight to follow him
03 · Dolby → Walk of Fame → Capitol → Sign

Landmarks cycle

The LED moves through landmark POVs. The tourists react — photographing the Hollywood Sign, miming shock at the stars underfoot, posing under Capitol Records.

SWIPE → cycle through the landmarks
↔
04 · Mellow · Street-level faces

The pivot

Music softens. "I want you to look deeper." The LED lingers on real faces — the fruit vendor, the auditioning woman. Cameras drop. Maps fold.

PRESS & HOLD — look deeper
Hold
05 · Pavement → Soil → Roots

The descent

"Past the pavement, past the glamour, down into the very soil." Concrete cracks. Earth. Roots.

DRAG ↓ down through the layers
↓
06 · Dark · Silence

Hold the silence

WAIT for the silence to pass
07 · Lights up · Dance · Video · Audio

The number

Five dancers revealed in a line. Video on the wall, song over the speakers. The dancers perform — no live poets, no live band, just video, audio, movement.

TAP each dancer (5)
08 · Wash · Path lit

To the tunnel

The Tour Guide returns. "Alright folks! Follow me…" A lit path on the floor draws the audience to the left.

TAP the lit path
Space 02

Boyle Heights

A warehouse tianguis. The youth poet sings; the elder walks in from the mural. They meet in the middle.

01 · Procession · Warm wash

The tunnel opens

You smell the tianguis before you see it — spice, carne asada, cilantro.

PRESS & HOLD — breathe it in
Inhale
02 · Scenic walk · Dancers as vendors

Through the market

"Welcome to Boyle Heights! Home of El Mercadito." Five tianguis stalls — each vendor a dancer in costume. Lowrider, taco stand, paletero cart.

TAP each vendor (5)
03 · Settle · Plaza wash

The Courtyard

Six stone tables ring the space. The band plays quietly on the stage to the north. A warm wash holds the square.

TAP a bench to settle in
04 · Tour Guide live

The speech

"Let's stop here in this courtyard for a moment… you are now standing in the epicenter of Los Angeles' Chicano culture. Stretching all the way from the iconic intersections of San Vicente & La Cienaga…"

PRESS & HOLD — listen
Listen
05 · Voice · Spot snaps up

The reveal

A voice from behind: "You mean San Vicente and La Cienaga." The Youth Poet. The audience turns.

DRAG → turn toward the band stage
↻
06 · Spot tightens · Band · Billboards live

Youth on the stage

The band softens. She begins her poem. Billboards alive — the 7-Eleven on Osborne, Mariachi Square, Van Nuys, Reseda, Sylmar, the 210.

PRESS & HOLD — transfixed
Listen
07 · Blackout on poet · Held silence

Her spot goes out

"We sprouted from the soil / And the soil remembers / Us." The band holds a chord. A beat of silence.

WAIT for the silence to pass
08 · Voice from mural · Audience 180°

Voice from the south

From the mural wall behind: "Los Angeles is a tianguis, a marvelous marketplace…" The audience turns 180°.

DRAG ← turn toward the mural
↺
09 · Elder walks · Lights chase

The elder walks in

The Elder names each element as he passes — the lowrider lights up, the stalls glow, the merchandise catches color.

TAP each element as he names it (3)
10 · Audience parts · Youth descends · Elder finishes

Final words

The audience parts. The Youth Poet steps down from the band stage. He delivers the final lines from the center of the crowd — surrounded.

DRAG ←—→ part the crowd
