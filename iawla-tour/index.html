If I Awaken
in Los Angeles
Directed by Andrew Scoville · Composed by Derrick Hodge
Arts District Warehouse · Los Angeles · June 2026 — August 2027
Scroll ↓
The argument

Los Angeles has spent a century selling itself as a billboard. If I Awaken in Los Angeles is the show that walks behind the sign — through Boyle Heights and Koreatown, Leimert Park and Laurel Canyon, Filipinotown and the South Central church — and finds the city the postcards never printed.

The real LA is not on the hill.
It's in this room.

Why the scenic spend

The room is the show.

Every dollar of scenic design goes into something the audience walks through, not past. Seven distinct neighborhoods, built once and inhabited for fourteen months.

7
Built environments — each its own neighborhood, scenic language, crowd geometry.
110ft
LED wall in Space 01 — the longest single video surface in any LA immersive production.
150
Audience members per show, walking through together. The crowd is part of the staging.
14mo
Run length. The build amortizes over hundreds of performances, not a single tour stop.
Front-of-house · Pre-show · Zone 01

The arrival

Thirty minutes before curtain, the audience steps off the street into a Hollywood premiere — 75 feet of red carpet, a step-and-repeat backdrop, palm trees and string lights, marquee bulbs overhead. Every guest is photographed on entry. Those images return at the very end of the show.

Front-of-house · Pre-show · Zone 02

The bar & lounge

A 64-foot bar along the south wall. Sixteen stools. Vintage LA neon. Framed black-and-white neighborhood photographs. Tufted leather booths and a five-booth VIP section under brass stanchions. Audiences are impressed before the show even starts.

Space 01

Hollywood Boulevard

Hollywood Boulevard as a wide, shallow stage. The audience stands on the Walk of Fame. The wall is the street. The tour begins — and then breaks open.

What makes it important
The sidewalk is alive.

Five dancers arrive costumed as tourists — cameras, maps, fanny packs, phones held high. They greet and direct the audience at arrival, move through the sidewalk as Sekou speaks, and after the blackout, return in a line for the opening dance number. We are all tourists here, until the show decides we aren't anymore.

Space 02

Boyle Heights

A warehouse tianguis. Scenic elements grow larger as they move out from a central Courtyard ringed with stone tables. Youth sings from the band stage; elder walks in from the mural wall. They meet in the middle.

What makes it important
A courtyard with a horizon.

Audience enters from the bottom-right, led through tianguis stalls until they land in The Courtyard — a central plaza ringed by six stone tables. The band stage sits to their north; the mural wall is their horizon. Audience faces north for the youth poet, then turns 180° to face the elder.
