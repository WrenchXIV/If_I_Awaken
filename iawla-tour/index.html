EXPERIMENT · PROMENADE-WALK
If I Awaken
in Los Angeles
Directed by Andrew Scoville · Composed by Derrick Hodge
Arts District Warehouse · Los Angeles · June 2026 — August 2027
Lobby · Pre-show

The arrival

Thirty minutes before curtain, the audience steps off the street into a Hollywood premiere, then drifts into a brick-and-velvet bar that already feels like the after-party.

Space 01

Hollywood Boulevard

A wide, shallow stage. The audience stands on the Walk of Fame. The wall is the street.

01 · House up · Tourists greet

Arrival

You enter from the lobby. The five dancers are already here, costumed as tourists — cameras around necks, fanny packs, maps, phones up. They warmly greet and direct you toward spots along the east wall. Without a word: we're all tourists here.

02 · Spot · TCL Chinese Theatre

Sekou enters

Spot finds the Tour Guide. "Welcome aboard, folks!" The wall pushes in on TCL Chinese Theatre — pagoda facade, cement handprints. Hollywood Swinging. The dancers take selfies, consult maps, point at landmarks on the LED.

03 · Dolby → Walk of Fame → Capitol → Sign

Landmarks cycle

The LED moves through landmark POVs. The tourists react — photographing the Hollywood Sign, miming shock at the stars underfoot, posing under Capitol Records. The sidewalk is full of life.

04 · Mellow · Street-level faces · Slow

The pivot

Music softens. "I want you to look deeper." Landmarks recede. The LED lingers on real faces — the fruit vendor, the auditioning woman. Cameras drop. Maps fold. The tourists start actually looking.

05 · Pavement → Soil → Roots

The descent

"Past the pavement, past the glamour, down into the very soil." The LED pushes down through the sidewalk. Concrete cracks. Earth. Roots. Walk of Fame stars dim. The tourists stand still, arrested.

06 · Dark · Silence · Hold

Blackout

07 · Lights up · Dance · Video · Audio

The number

Five dancers revealed in a line, spread along the sidewalk between you and the LED wall — poised, ready. Video on the wall, song over the speakers. The dancers perform — no live poets, no live band, just video, audio, and movement.

08 · Wash · Path lit

To the tunnel

The Tour Guide returns. "Alright folks! Follow me…" A lit path on the floor draws the audience out of the room to the left, through a transition tunnel.

Space 02

Boyle Heights

A warehouse tianguis. Scenic elements grow larger as they move outward from a central courtyard. The youth poet sings; the elder walks in from the mural. They meet in the middle.

01 · Procession · Warm wash

The tunnel opens

You emerge from the tunnel into the east aisle. You smell the tianguis before you see it — spice, carne asada, cilantro. The Tour Guide leads.

02 · Scenic walk · Dancers as vendors

Through the market

"Welcome to Boyle Heights! Home of El Mercadito." Five tianguis stalls — each vendor played by a dancer in costume. Lowrider, taco stand, paletero cart. The scenic gets smaller as you move inward.

03 · Settle · Plaza wash

The Courtyard

Six stone tables ring the space — round pink-stone tops on green pedestals, with curved concrete benches. The band plays quietly on the stage to the north. A warm wash holds the square.

04 · Tour Guide live

The speech

"Let's stop here in this courtyard for a moment… you are now standing in the epicenter of Los Angeles' Chicano culture. Stretching all the way from the iconic intersections of San Vicente & La Cienaga…" (He struggles to pronounce them.)

05 · Voice · Spot snaps up · Banter

The reveal

A voice from behind: "You mean San Vicente and La Cienaga." The spotlight on the band stage snaps up — the Youth Poet has been there all along. They volley — her sharp, him sheepish. He cedes the stage and exits.

06 · Spot tightens · Band · Billboards live

Youth on the stage

The band softens. She begins her poem. The two billboards flanking her come alive with images — the 7-Eleven on Osborne, Mariachi Square, Van Nuys Blvd, Reseda, Sylmar, the 210 freeway curving through the hills.

07 · Blackout on poet · Held silence

Her spot goes out

"We sprouted from the soil / And the soil remembers / Us." Her spot snaps out. The billboards go dark. The band holds a chord. A beat of silence.

08 · Voice from mural · Audience 180°

Voice from the south

From the mural wall behind: "Los Angeles is a tianguis, a marvelous marketplace…" The audience turns 180°. A follow spot finds the Elder Poet at the base of the mural.

09 · Elder walks · Lights chase

The elder walks in

The Elder walks forward through the scenic elements as he names them. The lowrider lights up — "lowriding is where cars become moving museums." The tianguis stalls glow — "where wisdom, song, and teachings go with the tamales."

10 · Audience parts · Youth descends · Elder finishes

Final words

The audience parts and he walks into the middle of the Courtyard. The Youth Poet steps down from the band stage and joins him. He delivers the final lines from the center of the crowd — surrounded by the audience, Youth Poet beside him.
